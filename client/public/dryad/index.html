01 · Understanding

Dryad’s next identity should make its public value unmistakable.

Dryad has grown from its roots in ecology and research-community coordination into an international nonprofit data publisher serving science and medicine. Its own RFP describes the desired shift: from a scrappy startup identity toward the presence of a premier global data publisher.

CivicFirm understands this project as an evolution, not a break. The refreshed brand should retain the trust already associated with Dryad while better expressing the organization’s current scale, technical maturity, and hands-on service model.

Dryad’s public platform currently leads with the promise “Trusted, simple, community driven” and explains its value through curation, quality control, straightforward compliance, and nonprofit stewardship. That foundation is strong. The refresh should make those qualities easier to recognize at a glance across the logo, color system, typography, web templates, presentation materials, and social media assets.

Community interest

The brand should communicate that Dryad is a nonprofit service operating in the public and scholarly interest, not a purely transactional technology vendor.

Human curation

The system should make visible the hands-on expertise of Dryad’s curators and the support researchers receive when publishing reusable data.

Technical integrity

The design language should feel rigorous, accessible, scalable, and appropriate for a trusted global research infrastructure organization.

Co-design disclaimer: Any visual directions referenced in this response are offered as starting points for discussion. CivicFirm’s process is collaborative and co-designed with Dryad’s leadership, communications staff, and technical team; no design direction would be treated as final until it has been tested against audience needs, accessibility requirements, brand strategy, and implementation constraints.
02 · Approach

A structured process that turns strategy into implementation-ready design.

Our approach is designed for stakeholder review, technical handoff, and accessibility from the beginning. Each phase produces usable artifacts and clear decision points so Dryad’s team can move confidently from discovery to implementation.

1

Discovery and alignment

We begin with a focused kickoff, stakeholder conversations, review of existing brand and website assets, audience mapping, and a shared definition of what the refreshed identity must accomplish.

2

Brand strategy foundation

We translate findings into a practical brand brief covering positioning, tone, visual principles, accessibility considerations, and criteria for evaluating creative directions.

3

Logo and identity exploration

We develop two to three concept routes for Dryad’s logo, color palette, typography, and graphic language, then refine the selected direction into a complete core identity system.

4

Accessible web template design

We design implementation-ready page templates for priority content types, with responsive behavior, component notes, accessibility guidance, and collaboration with Dryad’s in-house technical team.

5

Marketing templates and handoff

We create presentation and social media templates, finalize brand guidance, package source files, and support a handoff session so Dryad can implement and extend the system internally.

04 · Suggested timeline

A 10-week engagement with defined review points.

This timeline assumes timely access to relevant brand materials, existing website context, stakeholder availability, and consolidated feedback at each review milestone.

Week 1

Kickoff and discovery

Confirm goals, audiences, decision process, technical constraints, accessibility expectations, and success criteria.

Week 2

Brand strategy brief

Deliver positioning, voice, visual principles, and creative evaluation criteria for approval before design exploration.

Weeks 3–4

Logo and identity concepts

Present two to three identity routes with logo, palette, typography, and system implications.

Weeks 5–6

Selected identity refinement

Refine the chosen direction, test accessibility considerations, and prepare the core identity system.

Weeks 7–8

Web template design

Design priority page templates, responsive states, and reusable components for Dryad’s technical team.

Week 9

Marketing templates

Develop presentation and social templates aligned with the approved identity and web direction.

Week 10

Final handoff and QA support

Package assets, conduct a handoff meeting, and provide one review cycle for implementation questions.

CivicFirm is built for organizations whose work matters to communities.

CivicFirm’s work focuses on accessible, compliant, thoughtfully designed websites and digital systems for governments, nonprofits, public-good organizations, and community-serving institutions. Dryad can review selected public work on civicfirm.com, and we would be happy to schedule a short walkthrough of additional branding, identity, and visual-system work that is not fully represented in the public portfolio.

Accessibility and compliance fluency

CivicFirm’s services emphasize custom accessible websites built to WCAG 2.1 AA, ADA Title II, and Section 508-aligned expectations. For Dryad, that means accessibility is not a late-stage checklist; it is part of design strategy, color decisions, typography, templates, and handoff documentation.

Public-good and nonprofit context

CivicFirm is accustomed to work that must satisfy multiple stakeholders, procurement expectations, public trust, and long-term maintainability. That is a strong fit for a nonprofit research data publisher with communications, leadership, and technical stakeholders.

Brand and content strategy

The proposed work draws on CivicFirm’s service offering in brand and content strategy: developing voice, visual language, and information architecture that clearly communicate mission and public value.

Implementation-aware design

Because Dryad’s in-house team will implement the live site, our work will prioritize practical file organization, responsive templates, reusable components, and clear design notes rather than abstract presentation-only visuals.

06 · Bid

A fixed-fee engagement for a complete brand and template system.

The bid below is calibrated to be practical for a nonprofit brand refresh while still giving the work enough strategy, design exploration, accessibility review, and implementation handoff time to be done well. It can be adjusted if Dryad would like to expand or reduce the number of templates, concept rounds, or post-handoff support.

Proposed fixed fee
$22,500

USD, inclusive of discovery, brand identity, accessible web template design, marketing templates, source files, and handoff support as described in this proposal.

This figure is intended to be appropriately scoped: not inflated with full website implementation costs, but not underpriced in a way that would compromise strategy, accessibility, or design quality.

Included assumptions

The bid assumes one kickoff meeting, up to four stakeholder working sessions, two to three initial identity directions, one selected direction for refinement, the web and marketing templates listed above, two consolidated feedback rounds per major phase, organized design source files, one implementation-oriented handoff meeting, and one post-handoff QA review cycle.

Optional support: Post-project support, additional templates, implementation QA, or ongoing brand governance can be provided under a separate monthly support agreement or hourly authorization.

This proposal is valid for 30 days and assumes the project can begin within a mutually agreed schedule after selection.
